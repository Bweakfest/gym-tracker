What's actually different in v4

  • V-taper torso: shoulders go wide, waist genuinely narrows (not a tube), hips flare out properly. Athletic male proportions.
  • Tapered arms: bicep peak bulges out at the upper third, then narrows toward the elbow, brachioradialis bulge at the elbow, narrows again to a tighter wrist.
  • Tapered legs: thighs widest at the hip, narrow to the knee, calf bulges, narrows to ankle.
  • Single-shape muscles: each group (delt, bicep, tricep, quad, hamstring, calf) is one smooth path. The 3 deltoid heads / 3 tricep heads / quad sections are shown by internal dark groove lines instead of stacked overlapping shapes — no more blobby seams.
  • 3D shading: a top-down lighting gradient overlays the whole body so it reads as a 3D form, not flat paper.
  • Cleaner head + neck: smoother skull-to-jaw curve, neck visibly tapers OUT to meet the shoulders.
